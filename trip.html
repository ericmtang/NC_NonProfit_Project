VOYAGES

WHAT ARE THE LEEWARD YOUTH VOYAGES?

Leeward Youth Voyages offers a range of diverse trips for students focused on a variety of interests within a marine conservation focus. All of our trips are led by experts in the field and are crafted to expose students to both marine conservation and environmental justice efforts in various global regions

Sustainable Fisheries
Indonesia

Let us bring the oceans to life, in depth, for your learners!

  • Engage with local businesses and communities
  • Learn about the depletion of marine resources
  • Importance of MPA development, and methods for developing sustainable fisheries
  • Receive lessons in marine policy and learn how to take underwater surveys to measure ecological health of MPA’s

Red Coral Restoration
Spain

Let us bring the oceans to life, in depth, for your learners!

  • Learn about the degradation of their local coral populations
  • Opportunity to use their new SCUBA certification
  • Participate in local Coral growing efforts with partnering organizations.
  • Receive lessons in marine policy and learn how to take underwater surveys to measure ecological health of MPA’s

Manatee Recovery
Caribbean Sea

Let us bring the oceans to life, in depth, for your learners!

  • Receive SCUBA certification and encounter wild manatee
  • Learn about the causes that endangered the manatee population
  • Receive lessons on the environment impacts of decreasing manatee populations
  • Participate in the conservation efforts within the community to restore the species

"Human reliance on plastic is causing big problems in the worlds oceans. "Things people use on a day to day basis usually ends up in the ocean leading to pollution of waters and marine creatures. A recent study found fish in the great lakes in the United States have high doses of anti depressant medication like Prozac due to the huge reliance on anti depressants in the North America. There is the added problem of huge air pollution in countries like China and India because when the rain evaporates into the atmosphere it makes its way into the ocean."
